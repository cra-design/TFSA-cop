A TFSA may continue or it may end

What happens after a death of a TFSA holder will vary according to the situation. It is important to be aware of the following items in order to  understand what the potential tax implications may be.

  • The type of TFSA
  • The type of beneficiary(ies)
  • Whether any income was earned after the date of death
  • How long, after the date of death, before amounts are distributed to beneficiaries

Depending on how the items above apply to your situation, they may affect the following:

  • Whether the deceased's TFSA continues to exist or is considered to have ended
  • How income earned after the date of death can be reported and taxed
  • Whether a beneficiary can contribute amounts they receive to their own TFSA, and whether the contribution would affect their available TFSA contribution room

 

Different types of beneficiaries

TFSAs can have 2 types of beneficiaries.   
<dl – horiz without border>
Successor holder             A survivor who has been designate as a successor holder of the TFSA
Designated beneficiary                 A survivor (who is not a successor holder), children, former spouses or common law partners, and qualified donees   
</dl>
Determining the type of beneficiary is the first important step. The type of beneficiary can be determined by:

  • Designations that the deceased holder may have made in their TFSA contract
  • Terms of the deceased holder's will (if there is one)
  • Succession legislation in the province or territory of the deceased holder  

TFSA holders who want to change a beneficiary designation they made in the past should contact their TFSA issuer.
For more information:

  • What is a designated beneficiary

Beneficiary designation in Quebec

Quebec does not recognize       the designation of successor holder or beneficiary. However, a surviving spouse or common-law partner in Quebec may still benefit from the exempt contribution  process. 
Survivors in Quebec may make exempt contributions   <xh>
Quebec survivors may contribute the amounts they receive to their own TFSA without affecting their unused contribution room if:

  • the amounts are paid to the survivor no later than December 31 of the year following the date of death
  • the survivor files a Designation of an Exempt Contribution TFSA (RC240)  within 30 days of making the contribution

For more information: Beneficiaries who are survivors [H3 on If you are a designated beneficiary]
</xh>
 If there are no beneficiaries
If there is no successor holder or beneficiary designated in the TFSA contract or will, the TFSA property/assets is directed to the deceased holder’s estate and is distributed according to the terms of the will.
