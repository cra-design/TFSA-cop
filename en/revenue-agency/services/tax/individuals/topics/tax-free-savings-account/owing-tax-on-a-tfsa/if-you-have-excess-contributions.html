If you are taxed on excess TFSA amounts

If you over-contribute to your Tax-free Savings Account (TFSA) by contributing more than your available contribution room allows, you will have to pay tax on the excess amount.

On this page

  • How excess amounts are taxed
  • How to pay tax on an excess amount

How excess amounts are taxed

If you over-contribute to your TFSA at any time in the year, withdraw the excess amount as soon as possible. Any excess amount in your TFSA is taxable at a rate of 1% on the highest amount of excess per month, for as long it is in your account. 

For example, if you over-contribute $2,000 in June and remove it in September in the same year, you will owe $20 per month for June, July, August and September (a total of $80).  If you remove the excess amount later in June, the same month of the over-contribution, the 1% tax still applies, but you would only owe $20.

An excess TFSA amount can happen if you do any of the following:

  • miscalculate your available room and contribute too much
  • re-contribute a withdrawal in the same year and do not have enough contribution room
  • contribute to multiple TFSAs without tracking your total contribution amount

The CRA monitors TFSA contributions on an annual basis and will notify you if you have an excess amount. We may send you a letter or a notice of assessment (NOA) detailing the excess amount and the tax owed.

If you are a non-resident

The tax on excess amounts can be combined with the tax on non-resident contributions.

The CRA can impose two separate 1% monthly taxes on your TFSA if you contribute when you are a non-resident and you exceed your available contribution room.  

For more information: If you are taxed on non-resident contributions.

How to pay tax on an excess amount

Submit your TFSA Return, any additional forms, and your payment to the CRA by June 30 of the calendar year after the tax applies. Depending on the payment option you choose, you may submit your forms separately from your payment.

  1. Remove the excess amount

    Before you pay, withdraw the excess amount from your TFSA (if you have not already)

  2. Complete the required TFSA tax forms

    • TFSA Return (Form RC243)
    • Schedule A – Excess TFSA Amounts (RC243-SCH-A)

  3. Send your TFSA tax forms to the CRA

    • Submit online: Option 1

      Use “submit documents” in your CRA account to send your forms

    • Submit by mail: Option 2

      Mail your forms to:

      Sudbury Tax Centre

      TFSA Processing Unit
      Canada Revenue Agency
      Sudbury Tax Centre
      Post Office Box 20000, Station A
      Sudbury ON   P3A 5C1

      Winnipeg Tax Centre

      TFSA Processing Unit
      Canada Revenue Agency
      Winnipeg Tax Centre
      Post Office Box 14000, Station Main
      Winnipeg MB   R3C 3M2

      If you send your forms by mail, you may include your payment.

  4. Choose a payment method and submit the amount owing:

    Go to: Payment options

    1. Select Other benefit, tax, fee or account owing
    2. Select Yes
    3. Select An individual
    4. Select TFSA
    5. View your payment options

If you disagree with your assessment, you may request a tax waiver or cancellation.
