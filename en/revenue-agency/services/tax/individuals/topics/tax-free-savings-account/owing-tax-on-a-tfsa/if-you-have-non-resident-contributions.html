If you are taxed on non-resident TFSA contributions

Any contribution that you make to your Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) while you're a non-resident of Canada is taxable.

On this page

  • How non-resident contributions are taxed
  • If you have tax to pay

How non-resident contributions are taxed

If you contribute to your TFSA while you're a non-resident, withdraw the amount as soon as possible. Non-resident contributions are taxed at a rate of 1% per month for each month the contribution remains in the account.

This tax continues to apply until one of the following events happen (whichever comes first):

  • The month you withdraw the entire non-resident contribution from the TFSA
  • The month you become a resident of Canada again

Withdrawing only part of the non-resident contribution does not reduce your tax owing, you must remove all of it.

If you disagree with your assessment, you may request a tax waiver or cancellation.

If you have also over-contributed

The tax on non-resident contributions can be combined with the tax on excess amounts. If your non-resident contribution also exceeds your available TFSA contribution room, you'll be subject to both:

  • the 1% monthly tax on the non-resident contribution
  • the 1% monthly tax on the excess TFSA amount

For more information on taxable over-contributions: If you are taxed on excess amounts

How to pay tax on a non-resident contribution

Submit your TFSA Return and any additional forms to the CRA by June 30 of the calendar year after the tax applies.   Depending on the payment option you choose, you may submit your forms separately from your payment.

  1. Remove the non-resident contribution

    Before you pay, withdraw the entire non-resident contribution (if you have not already)

  2. Complete the required TFSA forms

    • Form RC243 – Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA) Return
    • Schedule B – Non-Resident Contributions to a TFSA (RC243-SCH-B)

  3. Send your TFSA forms to the CRA

    In your CRA account, use the Submit documents option to send us your TFSA Return and Schedule B – Non-resident Contributions to a TFSA (if not mailing by post)

  4. Choose a payment method and submit the amount owing

    Go to: Payment options for the type of payment you are making

    1. Select Other benefit, tax, fee or account owing
    2. Select Yes
    3. Select An individual
    4. Select TFSA  
    5. View your payment options 

    Note: if you choose to mail your payment by post, include your TFSA tax forms.

If you disagree with your assessment, you may request a tax waiver or cancellation.
