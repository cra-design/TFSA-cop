Calculate your room before you contribute

The most reliable way to know how much you can contribute to your Tax-free Savings Account (TFSA) is to calculate how much available contribution room you have.

Since your available room can change when you contribute or withdraw funds, it’s necessary to check exactly how much room you have available before you make a new contribution.

How contribution room changes

When you contribute to your TFSA, it immediately counts against your available contribution room. It will immediately reduce your available room by the same amount that you contributed.

When you withdraw from your TFSA, it counts towards next year’s available contribution room. It will increase your contribution room on January 1 of the next year by the same amount that you withdrew. The annual dollar limit will also be added to your available room at the start of the new year.

Since new contribution room only becomes available in the next year, you must be careful not to re-contribute a withdrawal in the same year unless you know you have the available room.

If you contribute more than your available room, you will be taxed on the excess amount.

Your own records are the most complete

We recommend that you calculate your available contribution room using your own records. Your financial institution and the CRA may not necessarily have your complete TFSA information.

Your financial institution may provide you with a report, but this will not be complete if you hold TFSAs with multiple issuers. The CRA’s information comes from records sent by TFSA issuers, but this is only updated once per year, usually in late February.

Your own records will provide the most complete calculation of your available room.

When your TFSA information is updated at the CRA

Your TFSA information in My Account is updated annually after we receive a report from your financial institution. They must submit a transaction summary by the end of February listing your TFSA contributions, transfers and withdrawals of the previous calendar year.

If you disagree with any of the information the CRA has on file, including dates or amounts of contributions or withdrawals that your TFSA issuer has provided to us, contact your TFSA issuer.

If any information provided by the issuer about your account is incorrect, the issuer must send us an amended report so that we can update our records.

When Moira accesses her CRA account on January 1, she does not realize that the TFSA contribution room does not reflect all the transactions she made the year before. This is because her financial institution has not yet submitted information about her transactions.

Financial institutions only send information about the previous year’s transactions at the end of February of the following year. Moira should have compared her transaction history from her financial institution with the transaction summary in her CRA account to verify her available contribution room.

How to calculate your room

Your TFSA contribution room is the total of the following:

The TFSA dollar limit of the current year
+
Any unused contribution room from previous years
=
Your available contribution room

Qualifying transfers, exempt contributions and specified distributions have no effect on your available contribution room.

Use the CRA worksheet or calculator

To calculate your contribution room for the current year, we recommend you either:

  • Use the PDF worksheet: Option 1

    Fill out the TFSA contribution room worksheet (fillable or printable)
    Form RC343, Worksheet - TFSA Contribution room

  • Use the My Account calculator : Option 2

    Sign in to My Account and use the contribution room calculator

    1. Sign in to your CRA account
    2. Select your Individual account
    3. Select Savings and pensions
    4. Select Contribution room
    5. Select Calculate your TFSA contribution room using your own records
    6. Select the button Do your own calculation
