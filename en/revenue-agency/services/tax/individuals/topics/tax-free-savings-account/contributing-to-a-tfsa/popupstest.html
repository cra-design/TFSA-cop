Before you contribute to a TFSA

Fair market value (FMV)

Is generally considered to mean the highest price expressed in terms of money that can be obtained in an open and unrestricted market between informed and prudent parties, who are dealing at arm's length and under no compulsion to buy or sell.

For information on the valuation of securities of closely-held corporations, see Information Circular IC89-3, Policy Statement on Business Equity Valuations.

Do not transfer from your RRSP

RRSP transfers count as income. If you transfer investments from your RRSP to your TFSA, you are considered to have withdrawn the investment from the RRSP at the fair market value (FMV). This can have serious tax consequences. The withdrawal counts as income and must be reported in your Income tax and benefit return for that year. You may claim the tax withheld on the withdrawal at line 43700 of your return.
